# Bitcoin Mining Economics: Why Miners Keep Mining at a Loss?

In the 2022 bear market, Bitcoin dropped from $69,000 to $15,500, a 77% decline.

**Electricity Cost vs Revenue Calculation:**
- Antminer S19 Pro hashrate: 110 TH/s
- Power consumption: 3,250W (78 kWh/day)
- Electricity ($0.10/kWh): $7.8/day

- **Daily loss: -$3.61**

Logic says shut down to stop losses, but network hashrate actually grew from 200 EH/s in June 2022 to 600 EH/s in 2024 (200% increase).

Why do miners keep mining at a loss?

**Deep Logic:**

Logic 1: Rational Version of Sunk Cost Fallacy
A miner bought an S19 Pro = $3,500 (was $12,000 in 2021 bull). Losing $3.61/day, but shutting down = 100% equipment depreciation. Keep mining = accumulate 0.00027 BTC daily. Miners bet: BTC will rise back to $40,000+, then this 0.00027 BTC = $10.8, turning today's loss into profit. Math model: When expected future BTC price Ã— daily output > electricity cost, rational choice is keep mining. 2022 survey: 73% of miners expect BTC to exceed $50,000 by 2025.

Logic 2: Difficulty Adjustment Mechanism's Prisoner's Dilemma
Bitcoin adjusts mining difficulty every 2,016 blocks (~14 days) to ensure average 10-minute blocks. If 30% of miners shut down due to losses, difficulty drops 30%, remaining 70% miners' output increases 43% (1/0.7=1.43). Result: Nobody dares shut down first, trapped in "prisoner's dilemma"â€”if I stop but you don't = you eat my share. Historical data: 2018 bear market difficulty max drop only -31%, proving most miners chose to endure.

Logic 3: Cash Flow Thinking vs Balance Sheet Thinking
Traditional businesses watch income statements (revenue - cost = loss). Miners watch balance sheets (BTC holdings Ã— future price). Example: Marathon Digital lost $366M in 2022 but accumulated 12,232 BTC. In 2024 at BTC $50,000, these BTC worth $612M, actual profit $246M.
