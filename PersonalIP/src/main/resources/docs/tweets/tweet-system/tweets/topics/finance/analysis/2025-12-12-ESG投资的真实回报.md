# The Real Returns of ESG Investing

ESG investing (Environmental, Social, Governance) faces a trust crisis in 2024: global ESG fund AUM dropped from $2.7 trillion peak in 2021 to $2.1 trillion in September 2024 (-22%), with three consecutive years of net redemptions. US market particularly brutal: ESG funds saw $14 billion net outflows in 2023, plus $8.7 billion outflows in first 9 months of 2024. Root cause: promised outperformance didn't materialize, greenwashing scandals proliferated, fees significantly higher than traditional indexes.

In-depth Analysis:

From long-term performance perspective, ESG delivered no excess returns. Morningstar data shows 2019-2024 five-year period, US large-cap ESG funds averaged 10.2% annualized return, while S&P 500 index ETF (like SPY) delivered 12.1%â€”ESG funds underperformed by 1.9 percentage points. Worse, ESG funds charge average 0.48% fees vs. traditional index funds' 0.03% (like VOO), paying 0.45% extra annually for lower returns. On $1 million invested over 5 years, ESG fund ends at $1.648 million, SPY at $1.782 millionâ€”a $134K difference.

From rating consistency perspective, ESG system has severe flaws. MIT 2022 study found same company's scores across 6 major ESG raters (MSCI, Sustainalytics, ISS, Refinitiv, Bloomberg, S&P Global) correlated only 0.54 (1.0 = perfect agreement). Compared to credit rating agencies' 0.92 correlation, ESG ratings are basically "random noise." Example: Tesla removed from S&P 500 ESG Index in 2022 (factory safety and racial discrimination allegations), but MSCI gave AAA highest rating. ExxonMobil held higher weight in some ESG funds than Apple, reasoning "strong governance structure."

From greenwashing scandal perspective, regulatory gaps enable false advertising. 2022 Deutsche Bank's DWS fined $25 million by SEC, admitted exaggerating ESG integration (claimed $137 billion assets managed to ESG standards, actually only $4 billion). 2022 BNY Mellon fined $1.5 million for similar false claims. 2023 Goldman Sachs Asset Management fined $4 million, its "ESG Emerging Markets Fund" heavily held Chinese fossil fuel companies. These cases are just the tipâ€”Morningstar 2023 report found among Europe's 1,200 self-labeled ESG funds, 37% failed minimum ESG standards.

**Key Points:**
â€¢ ESG premium disappeared, turned into "ESG discount." 2019-2021 ESG funds outperformed because tech stocks (naturally high ESG scores) dominated markets. But 2022-2024 energy/financial stock rebounds (traditionally low ESG sectors) caused ESG funds to significantly underperform due to sector bias. Essentially, ESG isn't alpha source, it's sector bet

â€¢ Fee chasm erodes long-term wealth. ESG funds average 0.48% fees, 16x traditional index funds' 0.03%. Even assuming identical returns, after 30 years on $1 million investment: traditional index fund grows to $7.61 million (assuming 8% annual return), ESG fund only $6.86 millionâ€”fees consume $750K (10% of wealth)

â€¢ ESG resembles "ethical consumption" not investment strategy. If you exclude fossil fuels, tobacco, defense due to values, that's personal choice, but don't expect outperformance. Data shows the most "sinful" Vice Index (tobacco, alcohol, gambling, defense) 2014-2024 annualized 13.7%, far exceeding S&P 500's 11.2%. Risk-adjusted returns also superior (Sharpe ratio 0.81 vs 0.73)

**Data Support:**
â€¢ S&P 500 ESG Index (launched 2019) vs S&P 500: 2019-2024 cumulative returns 63.4% vs 74.2%, ESG index underperformed by 10.8 percentage points. Key reason: ESG index excludes Tesla, Berkshire Hathaway and other strong performers with low ESG scores
â€¢ Morningstar Q2 2024 data: global ESG fund 3-year survival rate only 68%, far below traditional funds' 82%. During 2022-2023 bear market, 214 ESG funds liquidated or merged, representing $36 billion in assets
â€¢ Fee differential comparison: Vanguard ESG U.S. Stock ETF (ESGV) charges 0.09%, already lowest among ESG funds, but still 3x Vanguard Total Market Index (VTI) 0.03%. Actively managed ESG fund median fee reaches 1.02%, 1.4x actively managed traditional funds

**Investment Insights:**
1. Replace ESG funds with "negative screening + traditional index": If you insist on avoiding certain sectors (like fossil fuels), directly buy S&P 500 ETF then use shareholder voting to pressure companies on ESG improvements (BlackRock/Vanguard approach). Or choose low-fee indexes already excluding specific sectors, like SPYX (S&P 500 ex-Energy, 0.20% fee)

2. Beware "ESG packaging": Check fund prospectus pages 4-6 for actual holdings. If top 10 holdings are Apple, Microsoft, Amazon (identical to regular tech funds), don't pay extra for ESG label. Use tools like PortfolioVisualizer to compare ESG fund vs. benchmark index sector weightsâ€”if correlation >0.95, it's "fake ESG"

3. If you truly care about impact, consider Impact Investing not ESG: Invest in renewable energy project bonds, Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs), green municipal bonds. These instruments directly generate measurable environmental/social benefits, whereas ESG funds just "trade stocks in secondary market," minimal impact on corporate behavior (buying Apple stock doesn't make Apple plant more trees)

âš ï¸ **Risk Warning:**
ESG investing isn't a scam, but it's over-marketed and misused. If you choose ESG for values, accept potential performance drag and high fees. If you expect outperformance, historical data doesn't support this. Bigger risk is regulatory tightening: Republican-led US states (Texas, Florida) legislating bans on state pension ESG investments, EU tightening SFDR (Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation) standards. 2025 may see massive ESG fund "de-labeling" or liquidation wave.
