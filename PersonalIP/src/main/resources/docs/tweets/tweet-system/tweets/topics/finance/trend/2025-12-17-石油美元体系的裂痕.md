# Cracks in the Petrodollar System

Observed trend signals:

Signal 1: Saudi-China Yuan Oil Deals Breakthrough (March 2023)
In March 2023, Saudi Aramco and Sinopec signed the first yuan-denominated oil trade contract for 65 million barrels (valued at ~$6.5B). Throughout 2023, yuan settlement reached 17% of Saudi oil exports to China (about $34B), up from 0%. In H1 2024, this ratio further increased to 23% (annualized ~$52B). This breaks Saudi Arabia's tradition of 100% dollar settlement since 1974.

Saudi foreign reserve structure confirms the trend: dollar share fell from 78% (2020) to 68% (2023), yuan rose from 1% to 9%, euro from 15% to 18% (SAMA data). Saudi sovereign wealth fund PIF's China asset allocation grew from $12B (2020) to $38B (2023), up 217%.

Signal 2: Petrodollar Recycling Volume Sharp Decline
OPEC petrodollar reinvestment in U.S. Treasuries fell from $742B peak (2014) to $287B (2023), down 61%. Saudi Treasury holdings dropped from $184B (January 2020) to $135B (June 2024), down 27%; UAE from $64B to $41B, down 36%. Meanwhile, OPEC increased China bond holdings by ~$120B and European bonds by ~$68B.

More significantly, petrodollar "external circulation": in 2023, Gulf countries' oil revenues were allocated 42% to domestic infrastructure and sovereign funds, 28% to Asian non-U.S. assets, 17% to Europe, only 13% back to U.S. (IMF data). In 2010, this was 65% to U.S., 35% elsewhereâ€”a stark contrast.

Signal 3: Alternative Settlement Systems Accelerating
Russia-China achieved 95%+ local currency settlement in trade. Of 2023's $240.1B bilateral trade, yuan accounted for 67%, ruble 28%, USD only 5% (down from 33% in 2021). India-Russia established rupee-ruble settlement, processing $45B in 2023, completely bypassing the dollar.

BRICS expansion accelerates de-dollarization: January 2024 addition of Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iran, Egypt, Ethiopia, Argentina brings BRICS+ share to 42% of global oil production, 47% of natural gas, 38% of gold reserves. Brazilian President Lula proposed BRICS common currency during April 2023 China visit; working group now established.

Digital currency cross-border payment system (mBridge) progress: CBDC cross-border settlement platform involving China, Thailand, UAE, Saudi Arabia completed $16.4B pilot transactions in 2023, targeting $50B in 2024. Settlement time reduced from SWIFT's 2-5 days to seconds, costs from 0.3-1% to 0.02%.

Future outlook:

The petrodollar system won't collapse immediately but will face continued erosion. By 2030 projections:
- Global oil trade dollar settlement from current 80% to 55-60%
- Yuan share from current 17% to 25-30%
- Euro share from current 3% to 10-12%
- Other currencies and digital currencies from 0% to 3-5%

Dollar global payment share synchronously declines: from current 42% to below 35% (SWIFT data shows dollar fell from 43.5% in 2020 to 42.1% in 2023, while yuan rose from 1.9% to 4.7%).
