Phase 2: Learning Jobs-to-be-Done Methodology (Week 3)

  2. Existing alternatives: How did you solve this before?
  3. Switching costs: Why switch to our product?
  4. Success criteria: When do you consider it "successful"?
  5. Barriers: What almost made you give up?

Phase 3: Improved 25 Interviews (Week 4-6)

  Follow-up: "What happened?"
  Follow-up: "How did that make you feel?"
  Follow-up: "What action did you take?"

  Question: "Before using our product, how did you solve this problem?"
  Follow-up: "What was wrong with that method?"
  Follow-up: "Why didn't you continue using that approach?"
  Follow-up: "Did you try other tools? Why didn't you stick with them?"

  35-50 minutes: Understand switching decision
  Question: "What ultimately made you decide to try our product?"
  Follow-up: "What concerns did you have?"
  Follow-up: "What addressed those concerns?"
  Follow-up: "If you could do it over, what would you change?"

  Last 10 minutes: Uncover deeper needs
