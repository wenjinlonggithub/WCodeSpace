# Cognitive Bias: The Greatest Wealth Destroyer

During the 2008 financial crisis, Warren Buffett bought $5B of Goldman Sachs stock.

On the same day, retail investors were panic selling.

5 years later, Buffett earned 300%, retail investors lost 50%+.

Why did the same information lead to such different outcomes?

**Answer: Cognitive bias.**

Daniel Kahneman revealed in "Thinking, Fast and Slow": The human brain has two systemsâ€”

System 1: Fast, intuitive, emotional
System 2: Slow, rational, logical

95% of decisions are dominated by System 1, but investing requires System 2.

**Loss Aversion Bias:**
Research shows humans feel loss pain 2.5x stronger than gain pleasure.

This explains why:
- When stocks drop 10%, panic selling
- When stocks rise 10%, eager to take profits
- Buy high, sell low pattern

Kahneman experiment: 1000 people chooseâ€”
A: Guaranteed $500
B: 50% chance $1000, 50% chance $0

84% chose A (same expected value, but certainty preferred)

But when choices became:
A: Guaranteed loss $500
B: 50% chance lose $1000, 50% chance lose $0

69% chose B (knowing same expected value, risked more to avoid certain loss)

**This is the psychological root of retail "buy high, sell low."**

**Anchoring Bias Case:**

Tesla stock: January 2020 $85, November 2021 peak $414.

When it dropped to $200 in January 2022, countless investors thought "50% discount," bought heavily.

But $200 vs 2020's $85 was still 2.4x.

Anchored to $414, $200 looked like "bottom fishing," actually might be "bag holding."

Amos Tversky found: People unconsciously influenced by first number encountered.

**Confirmation Bias Power:**

Ray Dalio: "Most failures I've seen stem from only hearing what you want to hear."

Investors tend to:
- Only see positive news, ignore negative
- Only follow analysis supporting their views
- Mistake luck for skill, skill for luck

During 2000 dot-com bubble, Nasdaq crashed from 5048 to 1114.

But around 5000, 99% of analysts still shouted "buy."

Why? Confirmation biasâ€”they only saw information supporting "continued rise."

**Overconfidence Bias:**

Brad Barber and Terrance Odean studied 35,000 investment accounts:

More frequent trading = worse returns.

Most active 20% of investors earned 11% less annually than least active 20%.

Why? Overconfidence made people think they could "time the market," actually self-destructive.

**Real Case: Long-Term Capital Management**

1998 LTCM collapsed, lost $4.6B in 4 months.

Team included two Nobel Prize economists, top Wall Street traders.

Their models showed extremely low risk, but ignored "black swan" events.

Myron Scholes later admitted: "We were too confident in our models."

**Herd Mentality Cost:**

2017 Bitcoin mania, from $1000 to $20000.

Google searches for "how to buy bitcoin" peaked in December.

Retail investors flooded in, right at the top.

2018 Bitcoin fell to $3200, 80% of retail investors trapped.

**Why Do Smart People Make Mistakes?**

Nobel laureate Richard Thaler found: High IQ â‰  Good decisions.

Even knowing cognitive biases doesn't prevent them.

Because biases come from brain's survival instincts, not rational defects.

**How Buffett Fights Bias:**

1. **10-Year Rule**: Before buying stocks ask "willing to hold 10 years?" Avoids short-term thinking
2. **Contrarian Thinking**: Cautious when everyone's optimistic, optimistic when everyone's pessimistic
3. **Data-Driven**: Decisions based on financial data, not emotions or news
4. **Constraint Systems**: Set strict buy/sell standards, don't allow emotional influence

**Charlie Munger's Bias Checklist:**

Before every major decision, Munger checks 25 common cognitive biases:

â€¢ Confirmation bias: Am I only seeing supporting evidence?
â€¢ Anchoring bias: Am I influenced by irrelevant numbers?
â€¢ Loss aversion: Am I missing opportunities due to fear of loss?
â€¢ Herd mentality: Am I following others without thinking?
â€¢ Overconfidence: Am I overestimating my judgment ability?

**Practical Combat Methods:**

1. **Pre-commitment**: Set rules when emotions are calm, execute when emotions are high
   
   Example: If stock drops >20%, must review fundamentals, no emotional selling

2. **Reverse Confirmation**: Actively seek evidence opposing your view
   
   Question: If I were short, what reasons would I find?

3. **Time Isolation**: Delay major decisions 24-48 hours
   
   Write reasons when impulsive, reassess when calm

4. **Group Decisions**: Establish "devil's advocate" mechanism
   
   Have team members specifically challenge mainstream views

**Investment Masters' Common Trait:**

Not smarter, but better at controlling cognitive biases.

Peter Lynch: 29% annual returns for 9 years, secret was "invest in what you understand"â€”fighting overconfidence.

George Soros: Admits mistakes faster than anyoneâ€”fighting confirmation bias.

Ray Dalio: Built "Principles" system, replaced intuition with institutionsâ€”fighting emotional decisions.

**Ultimate Insight:**

Wealth isn't killed by markets, it's killed by your own brain.

Cognitive bias makes people do wrong things at wrong times.

True investment ability = Information processing Ã— Bias control

Most focus only on the former, ignore the latter.

But bias control is more important than information gathering, because same information with different cognitive frameworks yields completely different conclusions.

**Actionable Takeaways:**

1. **Build "Decision Journal"**: Record every investment decision's reasoning and emotional state, review after 3 months, identify your bias patterns

2. **Set up "Contrarian Account"**: Use small amount for investments opposite to main views, force yourself to notice contrary information

3. **Create "Bias Checklist"**: Spend 10 minutes before each investment checking 5 most common biases

4. **Seek "Cognitive Diversity"**: Regularly discuss with people having different views, avoid echo chambers

5. **Practice "Probabilistic Thinking"**: Don't say "definitely will rise," say "70% probability will rise"

Cognitive bias is human nature, can't be completely eliminated.

But acknowledging, identifying, controlling gives huge advantage.

Because most people will never do this.
