# Distributed Work is the End Point of Organizational Evolution

Pre-pandemic, remote work was seen as a "privilege."

Post-pandemic, remote work became the "norm."

But most people still haven't realized:

We're witnessing a fundamental transformation of human organizational forms.

Distributed work isn't a temporary solutionâ€”it's the **ultimate form** of organizational evolution.

**Three Stages of Organizational Evolution:**

**Stage 1: Geographic Centralization (1750-1950)**

Industrial Revolution spawned the factory model:
- Workers must work at fixed locations
- Management through physical supervision
- Information transferred face-to-face

Core logic: **Means of production couldn't move**

Machines, materials, tools were in factories; people had to come to machines.

**Stage 2: Knowledge Centralization (1950-2020)**

Information Revolution created the office model:
- Knowledge workers gathered in office buildings
- Management through meetings and reports
- Information transferred via computers and networks

Core logic: **Information density required concentration**

Complex decisions needed high-frequency communication; face-to-face was most efficient.

**Stage 3: Distributed Collaboration (2020-Future)**

Digital Revolution liberated work from physical constraints:
- Workers can be anywhere
- Management through data and results
- Collaboration through cloud and AI

Core logic: **Value creation decoupled from geographic location**

**Why is Distributed the End Point?**

Because it solves traditional organizations' fundamental contradiction: **Conflict between scale and efficiency**

Dunbar's number shows: Human effective social network limit is 150 people.
