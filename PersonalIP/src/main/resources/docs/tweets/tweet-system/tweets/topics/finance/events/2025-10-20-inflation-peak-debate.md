# Has Inflation Peaked? The Truth Behind the Data

CPI declined for 3 consecutive months
Markets celebrate "inflation peak" ğŸ‰

But is it true?

**Surface Data:**

CPI YoY:
- June: 9.1%
- Sept: 3.7%
- Dec: 3.1%

Looks beautiful

**Deep Data:**

Core CPI (ex-food & energy):
Still elevated at 4.5%

Shelter CPI:
Continuing to rise, 6-month lag

Wage Growth:
5.1%, above historical average

**My Assessment:**

Inflation declining â‰  Inflation peaked

Why:

1. **Base Effect**
   Last year too high
   This year looks better
   But absolute level still elevated

2. **Energy Price Volatility**
   Oil spike
   Inflation bounces immediately

3. **Service Inflation Sticky**
   Goods prices fall easily
   Service prices don't

**Historical Lesson:**

1970s:
Multiple "peaks"
Result: Kept rebounding

Fed Chair Volcker:
Had to hike to 20%
To truly defeat inflation

**Fed is only at 5.5% now**

**Investment Implications:**

If inflation truly peaked:
- Fed stops hiking
- Stock market rallies
- Bond bull market

If inflation rebounds:
- Continued hikes
- Stocks under pressure
- Cash is king

**My Positioning:**

Not betting one way
Prepare for both:

50% cash (protect against rebound)
30% stocks (bet on peak)
20% gold (hedge both sides)

**Remember:**

Market consensus often wrong
Independent thinking crucial

Data speaks
But you must understand what it says

Do you think inflation has peaked?
