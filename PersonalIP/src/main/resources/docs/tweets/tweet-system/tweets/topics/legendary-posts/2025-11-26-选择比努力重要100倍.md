# Choice Matters 100x More Than Effort

Conventional wisdom tells us: work hard enough, and you'll succeed.

But the truth might be the exact opposite.

**Why?**

Observation 1: Average Google engineer who joined in 2004 had $4.2M net worth by 2014; equally hardworking Blockbuster HQ employee who joined same year saw company bankruptcy in 2014, average net worth $180K. 23x difference, same effort level.

Observation 2: McKinsey 2023 study shows industry growth rate impacts personal income 87x more than individual effort. Choosing 30% annual growth AI industry vs 2% growth traditional manufacturing creates 6.7x income gap over 10 years.

Observation 3: Warren Buffett said, "When a management with a reputation for brilliance tackles a business with a reputation for bad economics, it is the reputation of the business that remains intact." His own wealth is 92% from choosing insurance and finance - industries with compounding effects.

**Root Cause:**

Compound interest and exponential growth don't care about your effort - only your base growth rate.

- Effort can improve you 10%-20%
- Choice can change your base growth rate from 2% to 30%
- After 10 years: 1.02^10 = 1.22 vs 1.30^10 = 13.79, an 11.3x gap

Math doesn't lie. On the wrong track, your effort just gets you faster to somewhere you don't want to be.

**Core Insights:**

ğŸ’¡ Effort optimizes execution efficiency (10%-30% gain), choice optimizes base growth rate (2X-10X difference)

ğŸ’¡ Being Top 1% in low-growth industry < Top 20% in high-growth industry (Data: 50th YouTube employee in 2006 > 100x wealth of top newspaper journalist from same period)

ğŸ’¡ Compounding's essence is time amplifying base rate - if base is negative or near zero, time becomes your enemy

**Underlying Logic:**

Charlie Munger's wisdom: "All I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there."

Don't ask "How can I do better?" Ask "Which track makes ordinary effort produce extraordinary results?"
