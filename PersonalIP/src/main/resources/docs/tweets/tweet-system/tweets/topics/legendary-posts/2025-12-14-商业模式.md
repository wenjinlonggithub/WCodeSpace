**Business Model Scoring Standards (Buffett + a16z Framework):**

Excellent business models must satisfy:

2. **Predictable recurring revenue (>70%):** Subscription, membership, consumable models

4. **Low CAC, high LTV (LTV/CAC >3)**
5. **Marginal cost â†’ 0:** Serving additional customer doesn't increase costs (software, platforms)

**Counterintuitive Insight:**

Traditional thinking: Scale first, monetize later.
Business model thinking: Without good business model, bigger scale = bigger losses (WeWork, early Uber Eats).

Jeff Bezos's counterintuitive: "Your margin is my opportunity." High-margin industries attract competitors. So Amazon deliberately maintains low margins (net margin 3-5%), building moat with "scale+efficiency," not "high pricing."

**How to Choose/Design Business Models:**

1. **Prioritize "Recurring Revenue" Models:**
   - Subscription > One-time sales
   - Consumables > Durables
   - Platform commission > Self-operated retail

2. **Pursue "Decreasing Marginal Cost":**
   - Software > Hardware
   - Digital products > Physical products
   - Knowledge products > Labor-intensive services

3. **Create "Customer Lock-in Mechanisms":**
   - Data lock-in (Salesforce)
   - Ecosystem lock-in (Apple)
   - Habit lock-in (social networks)
   - Network effect lock-in (Visa, WeChat)

**Actionable Steps:**

1. **If you're an entrepreneur:**
   - Don't ask "what product can I make," ask "what business model can I design"
   - Case: Slack isn't just better chat software, it's "pay per active user" business model innovation

2. **If you're an investor:**
   - Filter targets by "business model quality," prioritize SaaS, platforms, consumable models
   - Avoid low-margin, one-time revenue, asset-heavy models (unless extreme scale advantage)

3. **If you're in traditional industry:**
   - Think how to shift from "one-time transactions" to "ongoing relationships"
   - Case: Auto industry shifting from "selling cars" to "connected car subscription services" (Tesla FSD subscription)

**Ultimate Insight:**

Business model evolution direction:
- From "product" to "service" (Adobe Creative Suite â†’ Creative Cloud)
- From "transaction" to "relationship" (Amazon from selling books â†’ Prime membership ecosystem)
- From "ownership" to "access" (Zipcar, bike-sharing)
