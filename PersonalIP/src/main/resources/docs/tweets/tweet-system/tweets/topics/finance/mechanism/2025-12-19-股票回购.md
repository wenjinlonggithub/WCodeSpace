# Stock Buybacks: Shareholder Value or Financial Engineering

How stock buybacks work:

1. Mechanism essence: Companies use cash to repurchase their own shares from the open market or shareholders and cancel them, reducing share count. EPS formula is net income Ã· shares outstanding; when denominator decreases while numerator stays constant, EPS automatically rises. This is the simplest, most direct "financial optimization" method.

2. Implementation methods: Open market repurchases (92%): Companies buy at market price on secondary markets, usually in batches, offering flexibility but potentially pushing up share price; Tender offers (5%): Company sets fixed price or price range, makes offer to all shareholders who voluntarily choose whether to sell; Private negotiated repurchases (3%): Negotiated buybacks with specific large shareholders, common for founder exits or hostile takeover defenses.

3. Funding source analysis: S&P 500 companies' 2023 total buybacks of $959B sourced from operating cash flow 58% ($556B), debt financing 32% ($307B), asset sales 10% ($96B). During low-rate period (2020-2021), debt-funded buybacks reached 48%, with companies borrowing at 2-3% rates to repurchase shares, boosting EPS while increasing leverage risk.

Impact on investors:

Positive case: Apple (AAPL) repurchased $550B cumulatively from 2012-2023, reducing shares outstanding from 26.1B to 15.7B (down 40%). Over the same period, net income grew from $41.7B to $97B (up 133%), EPS from $1.58 to $6.13 (up 288%). Share price rose from $19 to $193 (up 917%). Buybacks contributed approximately 155 percentage points of EPS growth (288% - 133% = 155%), accounting for 54% of EPS increase.

Negative case: Boeing (BA) repurchased $43.4B from 2013-2019, spending 70% of free cash flow on buybacks instead of R&D. Result: 737 MAX crashes exposed design flaws, 2019-2020 losses of $11.8B, share price plunged from $440 to $89 (down 80%). Worse, buybacks depleted cash reserves, forcing company to seek $25B government bailout during pandemic.

EPS manipulation example: General Electric (GE) 2015 net income -$6.2B (loss), but through buybacks reduced shares from 10B to 9.1B, making EPS "improve" from -$0.62 to -$0.68 (still negative but smaller decline appearance). Same year executive compensation tied to EPS, CEO received $33M bonus, triggering shareholder lawsuits.

â€¢ Buyback motivation determines long-term effect: Buybacks funded by abundant free cash flow (like Apple) are shareholder returns; debt-funded buybacks (like GE) are short-term window dressingâ€”must distinguish company cash flow status and debt levels

4. Three red flags for buyback traps:
