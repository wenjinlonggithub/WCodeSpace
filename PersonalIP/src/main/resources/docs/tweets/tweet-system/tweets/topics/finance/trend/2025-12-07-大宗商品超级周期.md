# Commodity Supercycle: Is It Different This Time

Observed trend signals:

Signal 1: Copper surged from March 2020 lows of $4,617/ton to $11,104/ton in May 2024, a 140% gain. Lithium was even more extreme - lithium carbonate rocketed from $6,000/ton in 2020 to $85,000/ton in November 2022 (1,317% surge), then retreated to $11,200/ton by October 2024.

Signal 2: During China's 2000-2011 supercycle, the country consumed 52% of global copper, 54% of iron ore, and 60% of cement annually. China's 2024 GDP growth slowed to 4.8%, with traditional commodity demand peaking, but green transition creates new demand: EVs use 83kg of copper per vehicle vs. 23kg for ICE vehicles.

Signal 3: Energy transition drives explosive growth in critical mineral demand. The IEA projects that by 2040, clean energy technologies will increase lithium demand 42x, cobalt 21x, nickel 19x, and copper 2x. However, new mine development cycles take 7-15 years, creating obvious supply bottlenecks.

Future outlook:

This commodity cycle differs fundamentally from previous ones. 2000-2011 was China's urbanization-driven "construction cycle" dominated by iron ore, coal, and cement. 2020-2035 is a global green transition-driven "energy cycle" dominated by copper, lithium, cobalt, nickel, and rare earths. The key difference: construction cycles have endpoints (urbanization completes), while energy cycles represent structural transformation (2050 carbon neutrality targets).

More critical is the supply-side dilemma. Copper ore grades declined from 1.5% in 1990 to 0.6% in 2024, doubling extraction costs. Lithium mines take an average 12 years from exploration to production. Chile (world's largest copper producer) and Australia (world's largest lithium producer) face increasingly strict environmental approvals, making new mine development exponentially harder.
