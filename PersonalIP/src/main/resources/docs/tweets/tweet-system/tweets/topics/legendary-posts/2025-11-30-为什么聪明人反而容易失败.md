# Why Smart People Often Fail

Conventional wisdom tells us: higher IQ, higher success probability.

But the truth might be the exact opposite.

**Why?**

Observation 1: 2019 Goldman Sachs study tracked 500 Harvard MBA graduates (average IQ 135+) over 20-year careers, found only 23% eventually earned >$500K annually, while 77% earned less than similar-IQ entrepreneurs without elite degrees. Core difference: over-analysis led to action paralysis.
