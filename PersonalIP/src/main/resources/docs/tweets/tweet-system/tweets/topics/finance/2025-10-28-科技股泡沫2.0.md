## English Version

# Tech Bubble 2.0: Will History Repeat?

Tech stock valuations hit new highs, but is it truly different from 2000? The data tells the story.

**"Magnificent 7" Market Cap Concentration at Record:**

October 2024 data shows the seven tech giants (Apple, Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Nvidia, Meta, Tesla) combined market cap reached $14.8 trillion, representing 30.2% of S&P 500 total market cap.

Historical comparison is shocking:
- 1999 Internet bubble peak: Top 7 tech stocks were 23.1% of S&P 500
- 2024: 30.2%
- Higher concentration: +7.1 percentage points

Single stock influence:
- October 2024, Apple market cap: $3.48 trillion (7.1% of S&P 500)
- Comparison: March 2000, Microsoft $619B (3.8% of S&P 500)
- Microsoft was the world's largest then, but less influential than Apple today

Risk warning:
- If Magnificent 7 simultaneously drop 20%, S&P 500 would fall ~6%
- 2023 example: July-October, Magnificent 7 averaged -13.2%, dragging S&P 500 down 7.2%
- Higher concentration = greater systemic risk

**Valuation Comparison: P/S Ratio Insights**

Price-to-Sales (P/S) is key tech valuation metric (as many companies were unprofitable):

2000 Internet bubble P/S ratios:
- Cisco: 30.2x (peak)
- Yahoo: 100+x (1999)
- Pets.com: 73x (at IPO)
- Amazon: 27.5x (March 2000)

2024 "Magnificent 7" P/S ratios:
- Nvidia: 28.3x (high valuation but earnings-supported)
- Tesla: 9.8x
- Meta: 9.2x
- Amazon: 3.8x (vs 27.5x in 2000!)
- Microsoft: 13.4x
- Apple: 8.9x
- Alphabet: 6.7x

Overall average: 11.4x (2000 tech average: 45+x)

Key difference: Valuations relatively reasonable, but absolute levels still above historical mean (S&P 500 long-term average P/S ~2.5x).

**Profitability: Night and Day Difference**

The biggest 2000 vs 2024 differenceâ€”profitability:

2000 Internet company profitability:
- Amazon: Loss -$1.41B (revenue $2.76B)
- Pets.com: Loss -$147M (revenue $62M)
- Webvan: Loss -$453M (revenue $395M)
- Internet sector overall: 80%+ companies losing money
- Mantra: "Grab market share first, profits later"

2024 Magnificent 7 profitability (FY2023 data):
- Apple: Net profit $97.0B (margin 25.3%)
- Microsoft: Net profit $72.4B (margin 34.1%)
- Alphabet: Net profit $73.8B (margin 26.3%)
- Meta: Net profit $39.1B (margin 28.8%)
- Amazon: Net profit $30.4B (margin 6.3%, but AWS margin 24%+)
- Nvidia: Net profit $29.8B (margin 48.9%, stunning!)
- Tesla: Net profit $15.0B (margin 15.5%)

Total net profit: $357.5B, all profitable, most with margins >20%.

This is the fundamental difference: 2000 was "concept-driven," 2024 is "profit-driven."

**Free Cash Flow: Real Money Test**

Free Cash Flow (FCF) is the gold standard of true earning power:

2000 Internet company FCF:
- Most were negative
- Amazon 2000 FCF: -$278M
- eBay was rare exception: FCF $300M+ (thus survived bubble burst)

2024 Magnificent 7 Free Cash Flow (2023):
- Apple: $99.6B
- Microsoft: $56.7B
- Alphabet: $69.5B
- Meta: $43.0B
- Amazon: $21.4B
- Nvidia: $21.0B
- Tesla: $4.4B

Total: $315.6B, generating three hundred billion in real cash annually.

FCF yield comparison (FCF/market cap):
- 2024 Magnificent 7 average: 2.1%
- Not high, but at least positive
- 2000 most tech stocks: Negative or near zero

**Buybacks vs Cash Burn: Capital Allocation Divide**

2000 Internet company capital allocation:

**Investment Strategy: Navigating Uncertainty**

1. **Valuation Discipline**
   - Avoid tech stocks with P/S>15 or P/E>50 (unless extremely strong growth visibility)
   - Focus on PEG ratio (P/E / growth rate): <1 undervalued, >2 overvalued
   - Among Magnificent 7, Meta (PEG 0.9) and Alphabet (PEG 1.1) relatively reasonable

2. **Diversification**
   - Tech allocation max 35% of portfolio (current S&P 500 weight 30.2%)
   - Avoid over-concentration in Magnificent 7 (consider equal-weight tech ETF like RSPT)
   - Increase non-tech sectors: Healthcare, Financials, Industrials

3. **Quality Screening Criteria**
   - Free cash flow positive and growing
   - Net profit margin >15%
   - Debt ratio <50% (tech companies ideally <30%)
   - Recurring revenue >50% (SaaS model beats one-time sales)

4. **Stop-Loss Discipline**
   - If Magnificent 7 overall P/E rises to 60+ (similar to 2000 level), reduce position 50%
   - Individual stock down >25%: Review fundamentals, if no deterioration can add, if deteriorating stop loss
   - Overall market valuation: S&P 500 Shiller P/E >35, reduce equity position to 50% (current 32.8)

**Reality Check: Bubble or Reasonable Premium?**

Comprehensive judgment:

Bubble-like signs:
- Valuations at historic highs
- Market cap concentration at record
- AI theme over-hyped (small companies add "AI" to name and surge)

Evidence differing from bubble:
- Earnings real and strong
- Free cash flow healthy
- Stock buybacks not dilution
- IPO market cool (not frenzy)
- Retail participation cooled

Conclusion: **This is "premium" not "bubble,"** but premium level already elevated, margin of safety narrowed. If rates rise or recession hits, correction could reach 30-40% (not the 80-90% crash of 2000).
