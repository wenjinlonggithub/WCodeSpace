# Pricing Strategy: I Tested 20 Pricing Models

I spent 8 months testing 20 pricing strategies, changed from $9/month to $49/month, reduced churn, and tripled MRR. Most counter-intuitive finding: after raising prices, paid users actually grew 62%. Complete pricing psychology playbook.

My Solution:

**Background:**
â€¢ Initial pricing $9/month (thought cheap = easy sell)
â€¢ 8.3% paid conversion, but only $2,100 MRR
â€¢ Mixed user quality, 18% monthly churn rate
â€¢ Payment processing fees 12% of revenue, thin margins
â€¢ Goal: Find sweet spot balancing revenue and growth

**Execution Process:**

Phase 1: User Value Research (3 weeks)
- Conducted 45 user interviews asking "how much would you pay"
- Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter (PSM analysis):
  Too cheap (quality concerns): $7
  Cheap (good value): $18
  Getting expensive (need to think): $45
  Too expensive (would never buy): $89
  Optimal price range: $18-$45
- Calculated user time savings: 5 hours/month Ã— $50/hour = $250 value
- Discovered my $9 pricing far below perceived value (leaving money on table)
- Learned users associate higher price with higher quality

Phase 2: Competitive Pricing Analysis
- Researched 23 competitors' pricing strategies:
  Basic tier: $12-$29/month
  Pro tier: $39-$79/month
  Enterprise: $99-$299/month
- Industry standard $29-$49, my $9 significantly underpriced
- Feature comparison showed my product matches $49 competitors
- Analyzed CAC: realized low pricing couldn't cover marketing costs
- Calculated unit economics: need min $30 LTV to sustain growth

Phase 3: Pricing Experiments (5 months)
- Experiment 1: Price anchoring test
  Plan A: Single price $19 â†’ 9.2% conversion, $3,800 MRR
  Plan B: Three tiers $9/$29/$79 â†’ 10.1% conversion, $5,200 MRR
  Conclusion: Multiple tiers provide choice, most pick middle (decoy effect)

- Experiment 2: Price doubling test (boldest move)
  Raised from $9 to $29 (222% increase)
  Expected: Conversion rate crash
  Actual: Conversion dropped from 8.3% to 6.7% (only 19% decline)
  MRR jumped from $2,100 to $5,600 (167% increase)
  User quality improved, churn dropped from 18% to 9%
  Support tickets decreased 34% (better users)

- Experiment 3: Annual discount optimization
  Monthly $29 vs Annual $290 ($24.16/month, 20% off)
  Annual take rate: 28%
  Cash flow improved: 12 months upfront
  Churn reduced: Annual users renew 47% more

- Experiment 4: Feature-tiered pricing
  Starter $29: Basic features, 3 users
  Professional $59: Advanced features, 10 users
  Business $129: Full features + API, unlimited users
  Result: 62% chose Professional (middle option bias)
  Average revenue per user (ARPU) increased from $29 to $54

- Experiment 5: Usage-based pricing
  Charged per API call: $0.01/call
  Conversion increased 12% (lower barrier to entry)
  But MRR unstable, hard to forecast revenue
  Customer confusion about final bill
  Abandoned, returned to subscription model

Phase 4: Psychological Pricing Tactics
- Implemented charm pricing: $49 â†’ $47 (4% less, feels much cheaper)
  Conversion increased 3.2% (psychological effect clear)
- Added "Most Popular" badge to middle tier
  Selection rate jumped from 31% to 62%
- Displayed annual price first ($290/year), then showed monthly rate ($24/month)
  Annual selection increased from 28% to 41%
- Added value comparison: "$47/month vs 2 hours consulting at $200"
  Users more willing to accept pricing
- Used round numbers for annual ($300 vs $290), charm for monthly ($47)
