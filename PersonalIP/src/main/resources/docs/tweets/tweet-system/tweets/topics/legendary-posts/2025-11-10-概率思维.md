**Kelly Criterion: Optimal Bet Size**

Developed by Edward Thorp (mathematician who beat casinos) and Claude Shannon (information theory father):

**f* = (bp - q) / b**

Where:
- f* = Percentage of capital to invest
- b = Odds (multiples you can earn)
- p = Win rate
- q = Loss rate (1-p)

**Application Example:**

Suppose you have an investment opportunity:
- 60% probability earn 100% (double)
- 40% probability lose 50%

Kelly formula calculation:
- b = 1 (earn 100% = 1x)
- p = 0.6
- q = 0.4
- f* = (1Ã—0.6 - 0.4) / 1 = 0.2 (20%)

**Conclusion:** Should invest 20% of total capital, not all-in or nothing.
