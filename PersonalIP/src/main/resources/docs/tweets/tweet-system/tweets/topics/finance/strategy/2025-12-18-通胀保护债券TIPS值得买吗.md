# Are TIPS Worth Buying

Strategy Framework:

Step 1: Calculate Breakeven Inflation Rate
As of October 2024, 10-year TIPS real yield is 2.13%, while 10-year nominal Treasury yield is 4.35%, giving breakeven inflation rate = 4.35% - 2.13% = 2.22%. This means TIPS will outperform nominal Treasuries if average inflation exceeds 2.22% over the next 10 years. Fed's long-term inflation target is 2%, so current breakeven of 2.22% is slightly above target, representing relatively fair pricing.

Historical comparison: In March 2022 at inflation peak, breakeven reached 3.05% (10-year Treasury 2.35%, TIPS real yield -0.70%), fully pricing in inflation expectationsâ€”poor entry point. During March 2020 pandemic panic, breakeven fell to 0.55% (Treasury 0.70%, TIPS 0.15%), an excellent buying opportunity; subsequent 2020-2022 inflation surge saw TIPS outperform significantly.

Step 2: Evaluate Performance in Different Inflation Scenarios
High inflation scenario (2021-2023): CPI averaged 5.6% annually, 10-year TIPS total return -2.8% (purchased September 2021, held to September 2023), while nominal Treasuries returned -15.3%. Though TIPS had negative absolute return (bond prices fell as rates rose), they outperformed nominal Treasuries by 12.5 percentage points, providing effective inflation protection.

Low inflation scenario (2015-2019): CPI averaged 1.8% annually, 10-year TIPS total return +18.6% (purchased January 2015, held to December 2019), nominal Treasuries +21.2%. TIPS underperformed by 2.6 points, as actual inflation fell below the 2.1% breakeven rate at purchase.

Deflation protection: TIPS feature deflation floorâ€”principal repayment at maturity never below $100 par value. During 2008-2009 financial crisis when CPI briefly turned negative, TIPS principal remained protected, a unique advantage over nominal Treasuries.

Step 3: Consider Liquidity and Transaction Costs
TIPS market size: As of 2024, U.S. TIPS outstanding total $1.92T, only 7.1% of $27T total Treasury market, significantly less liquid than nominal Treasuries. 10-year TIPS average daily volume ~$5B versus $50B+ for nominal 10-year Treasuries (10x difference).

Bid-ask spreads: TIPS spreads run 0.08-0.12%, nominal Treasuries only 0.01-0.02%, making TIPS transaction costs 6-8x higher. Disadvantageous for frequent traders, but limited impact for buy-and-hold strategy (amortized to 0.01-0.012% annually over 10 years).

Individual investor channels: Direct TIPS purchase via TreasuryDirect.gov or brokers, $100 minimum. ETF options like SCHP (Schwab TIPS ETF, 0.04% expense ratio), TIP (iShares TIPS Bond ETF, 0.19% expense ratio) offer convenience but charge management fees. For investments under $10K, ETFs more suitable; above $100K, direct purchase reduces costs.

Key Points:

Four scenarios where TIPS allocation makes sense:
1. Expected inflation rising: Current 2.22% breakeven below your 10-year inflation forecast (e.g., expecting 3%+), making TIPS attractive
2. High inflation uncertainty: Geopolitical conflicts, energy crises, fiscal monetization increase inflation risks, TIPS provide insurance value
3. Long-term retirement funds: Retirees need protection against purchasing power erosion, TIPS guarantee 2.13% real yield, ensuring living standards maintained
4. Portfolio diversification: TIPS show low correlation with stocks (-0.15), 0.68 correlation with nominal Treasuries, reducing portfolio volatility

Situations where TIPS unsuitable:
1. Expected low inflation/deflation: Like Japan 1990s-present, prolonged deflation environment favors nominal Treasuries
2. Interest rate decline cycle: TIPS have similar duration sensitivity as nominal Treasuries to rate changes, but during rate cuts nominal Treasuries generate higher capital gains (due to higher coupon rates)
3. Short-term trading needs: Poor liquidity and wide spreads unsuitable for frequent trading
4. High return requirements: TIPS 2.13% real yield insufficient for high-return seekers, stocks or high-yield bonds more appropriate

**Key Points:**
â€¢ TIPS are inflation insurance, not high-yield instruments: 2.13% real yield guarantees purchasing power preservation, but absolute returns depend on inflation levels; even during high inflation, rising rates can cause mark-to-market losses
â€¢ Breakeven rate is core decision metric: Current 2.22% at historical median (2003-2024 average 2.18%), neither particularly cheap nor expensive, suitable for long-term allocation rather than tactical trading
