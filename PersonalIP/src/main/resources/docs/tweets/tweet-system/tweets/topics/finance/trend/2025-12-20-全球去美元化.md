# 全球去美元化：进程到哪一步了

## 🇨🇳 中文版

美元霸权的黄昏？数据显示去美元化正在加速，但步伐比大多数人想象的更复杂。

**美元储备份额的下降趋势：**

国际货币基金组织（IMF）数据显示，美元在全球外汇储备中的占比从2000年的71.5%下降到2023年第四季度的58.4%，20年间下降了13.1个百分点。这是一个渐进但持续的趋势。

更引人注目的是速度变化：
- 2015-2020年：每年平均下降0.4个百分点
- 2020-2023年：每年平均下降1.2个百分点
- 加速幅度：3倍

**中俄双边贸易本币结算案例：**

2023年中俄双边贸易额达到2401亿美元，创历史新高。其中本币结算占比达到95%以上，几乎完全绕过美元。具体数据：
- 人民币结算：约60%
- 卢布结算：约35%
- 美元结算：不足5%

这与2014年相比发生巨变：当时美元结算仍占90%以上。转折点是2022年俄乌冲突后西方制裁，迫使俄罗斯加速去美元化。俄罗斯银行数据显示，2022年2月至12月，中俄贸易中美元使用率从86%暴跌至不足20%。

**各国央行黄金购买激增：**

世界黄金协会（World Gold Council）数据显示，2022年全球央行黄金净购买量达到1,136吨，创55年来最高纪录。2023年继续保持高位，达到1,037吨。

主要购买国（2023年数据）：
- 中国人民银行：225吨（连续13个月增持）
- 土耳其央行：148吨
- 印度储备银行：102吨
- 波兰央行：57吨
- 新加坡金管局：77吨

这些央行的共同特点：都在积极减持美元储备。中国外汇储备中美元资产占比从2016年的65%下降到2023年的54%（据外汇管理局数据估算）。

**SWIFT vs CIPS的竞争格局：**

人民币跨境支付系统（CIPS）日均交易量数据：
- 2019年：350亿美元
- 2021年：520亿美元
- 2023年：890亿美元

增长率：154%（4年）

虽然SWIFT日均交易量仍高达5-6万亿美元，CIPS只占1.5%左右，但增速惊人。CIPS的参与机构从2019年的96家增加到2023年的1,373家，覆盖全球103个国家和地区。

沙特阿拉伯在2023年3月宣布考虑使用人民币结算部分石油贸易，标志着"石油美元"体系的潜在松动。虽然截至2024年初实际执行量有限（估计不足3%），但象征意义重大。

**投资策略建议：**

1. **货币配置多元化**
   - 美元仍需保持40-50%（短期内霸权地位难撼动）
   - 人民币配置提升至15-20%（从传统5-10%）
   - 黄金实物或ETF配置5-10%（对冲货币风险）
   - 其他货币（欧元、日元、瑞郎）25-30%

2. **关注受益资产**
   - 黄金矿业公司（Barrick Gold, Newmont）：央行需求支撑
   - 新兴市场债券基金（本币计价）：收益率优势
   - 跨境支付技术公司：CIPS生态受益

3. **风险对冲工具**
   - 美元指数期货：对冲美元下行风险
   - 货币对冲基金：降低汇率波动
   - 多币种账户：Wise, HSBC Global Money

**现实评估：去美元化是马拉松，不是百米冲刺**

尽管趋势明显，美元在以下领域仍占绝对优势：
- 国际贸易计价：88%（BIS 2022年数据）
- 外汇交易：占一侧货币的88.5%（BIS 2022三年调查）
- 国际债券市场：62%（BIS 2023年数据）
- 离岸存款：59%（IMF 2023年数据）

完全去美元化可能需要20-30年，甚至更长。但趋势已经形成，投资者需要提前布局。

⚠️ **风险提示：**
汇率波动可能导致本币资产大幅波动。新兴市场货币存在流动性风险和政策风险。黄金价格受美联储政策、实际利率影响大。CIPS系统仍在发展阶段，技术和监管风险并存。本文仅供参考，不构成投资建议。投资者应根据自身风险承受能力谨慎决策。

## 🇬🇧 English Version

# Global De-dollarization: How Far Has It Come

Is the dollar hegemony ending? Data shows de-dollarization is accelerating, but the pace is more complex than most assume.

**Declining USD Reserve Share:**

IMF data reveals that the USD's share of global foreign exchange reserves fell from 71.5% in 2000 to 58.4% in Q4 2023, a 13.1 percentage point decline over 20 years. This represents a gradual but persistent trend.

More striking is the acceleration:
- 2015-2020: Average decline of 0.4 pp annually
- 2020-2023: Average decline of 1.2 pp annually
- Acceleration: 3x faster

**China-Russia Bilateral Trade Case Study:**

China-Russia bilateral trade reached $240.1 billion in 2023, hitting a record high. Local currency settlement exceeded 95%, almost entirely bypassing the dollar. Breakdown:
- RMB settlement: ~60%
- Ruble settlement: ~35%
- USD settlement: <5%

This contrasts sharply with 2014 when USD settlement still exceeded 90%. The turning point was February 2022 when Western sanctions after the Ukraine conflict forced Russia to accelerate de-dollarization. Bank of Russia data shows USD usage in China-Russia trade plummeted from 86% to under 20% between February and December 2022.

**Central Bank Gold Buying Surge:**

World Gold Council data shows 2022 global central bank net gold purchases hit 1,136 tons, the highest in 55 years. 2023 remained elevated at 1,037 tons.

Top buyers (2023 data):
- People's Bank of China: 225 tons (13 consecutive months of additions)
- Central Bank of Turkey: 148 tons
- Reserve Bank of India: 102 tons
- National Bank of Poland: 57 tons
- Monetary Authority of Singapore: 77 tons

Common thread: All actively reducing USD reserves. China's USD holdings as percentage of forex reserves fell from 65% in 2016 to an estimated 54% in 2023 (SAFE data estimates).

**SWIFT vs CIPS Competition:**

Cross-border Interbank Payment System (CIPS) daily average transaction volume:
- 2019: $35 billion
- 2021: $52 billion
- 2023: $89 billion

Growth rate: 154% (4 years)

While SWIFT still processes $5-6 trillion daily, CIPS represents only 1.5%, but growth is dramatic. CIPS participating institutions grew from 96 in 2019 to 1,373 in 2023, covering 103 countries and regions globally.

Saudi Arabia's March 2023 announcement considering RMB settlement for part of oil trade signals potential "petrodollar" system erosion. While actual execution remains limited as of early 2024 (estimated under 3%), the symbolic significance is substantial.

**Investment Strategy Recommendations:**

1. **Diversified Currency Allocation**
   - USD: Maintain 40-50% (hegemony intact near-term)
   - RMB: Increase to 15-20% (from traditional 5-10%)
   - Gold physical/ETFs: 5-10% (currency risk hedge)
   - Other currencies (EUR, JPY, CHF): 25-30%

2. **Beneficiary Assets**
   - Gold mining companies (Barrick Gold, Newmont): Central bank demand support
   - Emerging market bond funds (local currency): Yield advantage
   - Cross-border payment tech companies: CIPS ecosystem beneficiaries

3. **Risk Hedging Tools**
   - USD Index futures: Hedge dollar downside risk
   - Currency-hedged funds: Reduce FX volatility
   - Multi-currency accounts: Wise, HSBC Global Money

**Reality Check: De-dollarization is a Marathon, Not a Sprint**

Despite clear trends, USD maintains absolute dominance in:
- International trade invoicing: 88% (BIS 2022 data)
- FX transactions: 88.5% of one side (BIS 2022 triennial survey)
- International bond market: 62% (BIS 2023 data)
- Offshore deposits: 59% (IMF 2023 data)

Complete de-dollarization may take 20-30 years or longer. But the trend is established, and investors should position accordingly.

⚠️ **Risk Warning:**
Exchange rate volatility can cause significant local currency asset fluctuations. Emerging market currencies carry liquidity and policy risks. Gold prices are heavily influenced by Fed policy and real interest rates. CIPS system remains developmental with technology and regulatory risks. This article is for informational purposes only, not investment advice. Investors should make prudent decisions based on individual risk tolerance.
