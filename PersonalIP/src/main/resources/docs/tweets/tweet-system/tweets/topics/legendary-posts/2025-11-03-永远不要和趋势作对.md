â€¢ Identify real trends vs false trends: Not all "new things" are trends. **Blockbuster vs Netflix: Entertainment Consumption Trend**

2000 Blockbuster was videotape rental king:
- 9,000 stores, $6B annual revenue
- 2000 had chance to acquire Netflix for $50M, CEO refused: "Streaming is niche market"
- 2004 Netflix launched subscription, Blockbuster followed (too late)
- 2007 Netflix launched streaming, Blockbuster thought "US internet too slow, impossible"

- Users don't want to drive to stores to rent discs (convenience trend)

**Detroit Big Three vs Tesla: Energy Transition Trend**

2010 GM, Ford, Chrysler views:
- EVs have short range (only 150 miles)
- Slow charging (8 hours)
- Expensive ($100K+)
- "Consumers won't buy EVs"

Their strategy: Continue investing in gas cars, EVs only for compliance (meeting government emission regulations).

Tesla's bet: EVs are irreversible trend
- 2012 Model S launch: 265-mile range (breaking "short range" perception)
- 2015 Supercharger launch: 80% charge in 30 minutes (solving slow charging)
- 2017 Model 3: starting $35,000 (reaching mass market price)

- 2015 global EV sales 550K units (0.7% share)
- 2020 global EV sales 3.24M units (4.2% share)
- 2024 global EV sales 14M units (18% share)
