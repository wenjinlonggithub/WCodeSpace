# All Business Is Fundamentally Information Asymmetry

In 1995, real estate agents earned 6% commission because only they knew home prices.

In 2024, commission dropped to 2-3% because Zillow made prices transparent.

Why is all business fundamentally about information asymmetry?

Naval Ravikant: "Business is arbitrage in the friction of information flow." When information is perfectly symmetric, profits approach zeroâ€”that's the "perfect competition" theory in economics. The moment information gaps disappear is when profits disappear.

The data:
- 1990s average real estate agent commission: 6% (buyers had no idea of market prices, relied on agents)
- 2010 after Zillow and platforms emerged: commission dropped to 3-4% (price transparency reduced agent value by 50%)
- 2024 with online viewing + AI valuation: commission further dropped to 2-3%, some self-service transactions 0.5-1%
- NAR (National Association of Realtors) 2023 settlement $418M, admitted commission manipulation

In 30 years, information transparency reduced agent income by 60-80%. Not because they "did poorly"â€”the information gap was eliminated.

**Core Insights:**
â€¢ All intermediaries are fundamentally information asymmetry: From Taobao (connecting factories to consumers) to VCs (connecting capital to founders), from headhunters (connecting talent to companies) to brokers (connecting buyers to sellers). Charlie Munger: "Find bottlenecks in information flow, you find money-making opportunities." Intermediary value = size of information gap
â€¢ Internet is the terminator of information asymmetry: Every time the internet reduces information costs by 10%, a traditional intermediary industry faces existential crisis. Industries disappeared in 20 years: Travel agencies (replaced by Expedia/Ctrip), stockbrokers (replaced by Robinhood), newspapers (replaced by Google News), record stores (replaced by Spotify)
â€¢ New asymmetries constantly emerge: When old information gaps close, smart people create new information frontiers. 1990s was internet info gap, 2010s was mobile internet info gap, 2020s is AI and Web3 info gap. Information gaps always exist, just forms change

**Underlying Logic:**

Information value formula:
Profit = Information Gap Ã— Transaction Scale Ã— Monopoly Degree Ã— Time Sensitivity

Larger info gap, more frequent transactions, stronger monopoly, shorter time sensitivity = higher profits.

Case analysis:

**Stock Brokerage Industry Information Gap Collapse:**

1980s:
- Retail investors couldn't access real-time stock prices (huge info gap)
- Trading commission 1-2% per trade (high profits)
- Could only phone-order through brokers (monopoly channel)
- Average annual commission $5,000-$8,000/client

2000:
- Internet brokerages emerged (E*TRADE, Schwab)
- Commission dropped to $10-20/trade (99% decrease)
- Real-time quotes free (info gap shrank)

2019:
- Robinhood launched zero-commission trading (commission to zero)
- All mainstream brokers forced to follow
- Industry profit model completely changed: from commissions to payment for order flow (PFOF), interest income

Result: Traditional broker market cap evaporated 80%, Merrill Lynch acquired, industry reshuffled.

**Travel Agency Industry Information Gap Extinction:**

1990s travel agency value chain:
- Exclusive access to flight schedules, hotel availability (information monopoly)
- Customers had no idea of real ticket/hotel prices
- Intermediary commission 10-15%

2000s Expedia/Priceline/Ctrip rise:
- Flight/hotel price real-time comparison (information transparency)
- User self-service booking (disintermediation)
- Travel agency commission dropped to 5-8%

2024:
- Google Flights/Skyscanner global comparison (complete information transparency)
- Airbnb/Booking direct connection (disintermediation)
- Traditional travel agency market share dropped from 80% to 15%

China data: 2010 had 150K travel agencies, 2024 under 50Kâ€”2/3 disappeared in 10 years.

**But information asymmetry didn't disappearâ€”it just shifted:**

**New Information Frontier 1: Data and Algorithms**

Google doesn't sell informationâ€”it sells "ability to process information":
- User search intent (behavioral data)
- Advertiser bidding willingness (commercial data)
- Google uses algorithms to match both, capturing the spread

2023 Google ad revenue $237.5B, essentially "information matching" commission.

**New Information Frontier 2: Professional Knowledge Barriers**

McKinsey consulting fees $500K-$2M/project, not because clients "don't know," but "can't do":
- Industry best practices (knowledge info gap)
- Cross-industry cases (experience info gap)
- Systematic methodology (capability info gap)

2024 global consulting industry revenue $320B+, proving "professional knowledge" info gap still valuable.

**New Information Frontier 3: Time-Sensitive Information Gap**

High-frequency trading (HFT) essence:
- Fiber optics shortened NY-to-Chicago info transmission from 17ms to 13ms (4ms advantage)
- Michael Lewis's "Flash Boys" exposed: HFT firms earn $5B+ annually from this 4ms
- Pure information time-gap arbitrage

**Ultimate Form of Information Gap: Data Monopoly**

Facebook/Tencent/ByteDance moats:
- Not "selling information," but "monopolizing user behavior data"
- Advertisers must go through their platforms to reach users (information intermediary)
- Platforms own user profiles, interests, behaviors (information asymmetry)

2024 Meta ad revenue $135B, Tencent ad revenue $28Bâ€”both monetizing "data information gap."

Warren Buffett's moat theory is essentially "sustained information asymmetry":
- Coca-Cola: Consumers don't know cost is $0.05 but willing to pay $2 (brand info gap)
- GEICO insurance: Masters actuarial data, knows risk pricing better than customers (professional info gap)
- See's Candies: Consumers don't know other chocolate quality, only trust See's (quality info gap)

Paul Graham in "How to Make Wealth": "Wealth isn't moneyâ€”it's solving problems. And problems often stem from information asymmetryâ€”some know answers, others don't."

**Destroyed Information Gap Industries:**

1. Encyclopedia salespeople: Wikipedia free (Britannica stopped printing 2012)
2. Yellow Pages ads: Google search free (Yellow Pages from $14B market â†’ $0)
3. Video rental: Netflix streaming (Blockbuster bankrupt)
4. Taxi dispatch: Uber/Didi real-time matching (traditional call services obsolete)
5. Bank financial advisors: Robo-advisors (Betterment/Wealthfront fees from 1.5% to 0.25%)

**Fields with Still-Massive Information Gaps:**

1. Healthcare: Doctors hold diagnostic knowledge, patients completely dependent (huge info gap, AI challenging)
2. Legal services: Lawyers hold legal codes and precedents (large info gap, LegalZoom eroding low-end)
3. Education/training: Teachers hold knowledge and teaching methods (medium info gap, online education compressing)
4. B2B sales: Suppliers hold product specs, pricing power (large info gap, procurement platforms increasing transparency)
5. Luxury goods: Brands monopolize "scarcity" and "identity" information (info gap deliberately maintained)

Peter Thiel in "Zero to One": "Monopoly businesses claim fierce competition (hiding info gap), competitive businesses claim uniqueness (exaggerating info gap)."

True story: 2010, NYC startup Honest Buildings wanted to create "price transparency platform" for construction. Building contractors fiercely resisted because their profits depended entirely on "customers not knowing real costs." Platform ultimately failed because suppliers refused transparency. This proves: Information asymmetry isn't just business modelâ€”it's power structure.

**Actionable Takeaways:**
1. Identify your information advantage: Ask "In which field do I have information others don't?" Could be: insider industry experience, exclusive network, professional skills, data access, time-gap advantage. This is your competitive moat
2. Continuously build "information moat": Info gaps shrink with technologyâ€”must constantly invest in new information: Subscribe to industry reports ($5K-$50K/year), join expert networks (GLG, AlphaSights), conduct primary research, build information channels. Buffett reads 5-6 hours dailyâ€”building information advantage
3. Beware "information platformization" threats: If your business is essentially information intermediation, either vertically integrate (from "providing info" to "providing service") or build network effects (become platform), or internet will eliminate you. Real estate agents' way out: shift from "providing listing info" to "providing transaction services + legal consulting + loan assistance"
4. Leverage information asymmetry for entrepreneurship: Find industries where "information is still opaque"â€”B2B procurement, medical devices, industrial goods, cross-border trade. These fields have huge info gapsâ€”entrepreneurial opportunities
5. Redefine information gap in AI era: When ChatGPT makes "knowing information" free, real info gap lies in "how to use information"â€”judgment, execution, network resources. Future info gap is "Taste" and "Judgment," not "Knowledge"

Information gap is business's eternal truth. It won't disappearâ€”only change forms. Your task: Identify current info gaps, anticipate future info gaps, build your own information advantage.
